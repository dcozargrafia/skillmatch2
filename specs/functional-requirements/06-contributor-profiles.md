# 6. Contributor Profiles

## Purpose

Contributor profiles describe the skills, availability, interests, and collaboration context of people who want to contribute to NGO micro-projects.

Profiles help contributors present themselves and help NGOs make informed assignment decisions without turning SkillMatch into a résumé, hiring, or freelance marketplace.

Contributor profiles must support skills-based matching and guided collaboration while keeping personal information limited to what is useful for the platform mission.

---

## Profile Ownership

A contributor profile belongs to one Contributor account.

Contributors may create and edit their own contributor profile.

Administrators may view contributor profiles when needed for platform governance, moderation, support, or operational integrity.

NGOs may view contributor profiles only when the contributor has applied to one of their projects or is assigned to one of their projects.

Guests must not view private contributor profiles.

---

## Profile Requirements

A contributor profile should contain enough information for project matching and NGO review.

At minimum, a contributor profile should support:

* Display name
* Biography or short introduction
* Skills selected from the centralized skills catalog, when applicable
* Skill proficiency levels for selected skills
* Availability information
* Collaboration or contribution history

The MVP should avoid collecting unnecessary personal, academic, employment, or credential information.

Formal credentials, degrees, certifications, employment history, and portfolio-style résumé fields are Post-MVP unless explicitly introduced later.

---

## Skill Rules

Contributor skills must be selected from the centralized skills catalog managed by administrators.

Contributors must not create arbitrary skills from their profile.

A contributor is not required to select at least one skill before applying to projects.

This avoids blocking contributors whose real skills are not yet represented in the catalog.

Each selected contributor skill should include a proficiency level.

Skill proficiency levels should be simple and consistent across the platform.

Skill selections are used for matching, project discovery context, applicant review, and contributor self-presentation when available.

If a contributor has no selected skills, the application message, availability, and any other allowed profile context become more important for NGO review.

Removing or changing a skill should update future matching and profile visibility, but must not rewrite historical applications, assignments, deliverables, certificates, or activity records.

---

## Availability Rules

Contributor availability should help NGOs understand whether a contributor can reasonably participate in a project.

Availability may include lightweight information such as:

* General availability status
* Estimated weekly availability
* Preferred collaboration capacity

Availability is informational and must not impose platform participation limits.

Contributors may apply to and participate in multiple projects at the same time.

SkillMatch should provide visibility into workload and activity rather than block contributors from participating in multiple projects.

---

## Profile Completion Rules

A contributor may register before completing their full profile.

The platform should guide contributors to complete profile information that improves matching and project selection.

A contributor should have enough required profile information before applying to projects so NGOs can make an informed decision.

At minimum, applying to a project should require:

* Display name
* Availability information
* Application message

Selected skills improve matching and review quality, but are not required for application eligibility.

The MVP should avoid complex profile completion scoring unless explicitly introduced later.

---

## Visibility Rules

Contributor profile visibility depends on relationship and context.

Contributors can view and edit their own profile.

NGOs can view contributor profiles for:

* Contributors who applied to projects owned by the NGO.
* Contributors assigned to projects owned by the NGO.

Administrators can view contributor profiles when required for governance, moderation, support, or operational integrity.

Guests cannot view private contributor profiles.

Other contributors cannot view private contributor profiles by default.

Public contributor profiles are not part of the MVP unless explicitly introduced later.

---

## Collaboration History

Contributor profiles may show collaboration history relevant to trust and transparency.

MVP collaboration history may include:

* Projects assigned to the contributor
* Projects completed by the contributor
* Certificates earned by the contributor
* Relevant contribution outcomes

Collaboration history should be based on platform activity, not self-reported claims.

Reviews and ratings are Post-MVP.

---

## Editing Rules

Contributors may update their own profile information.

Profile updates should affect future discovery, matching, and applicant review.

Profile updates must not change historical workflow records.

If a contributor updates their profile after applying to a project, the NGO should see the current profile when reviewing the application.

The application itself must preserve its own submitted message and activity history.

---

## Activity History

Important contributor profile events should be recorded when relevant for accountability, moderation, or support.

Activity history may include events such as:

* Contributor profile created.
* Contributor profile updated.
* Skills updated.
* Availability updated.
* Contributor profile viewed by an administrator for governance reasons.

The actor who performs an action does not need to receive a notification for that same action, but the action should be recorded when relevant for traceability.

---

## Notifications

The platform should notify relevant users when:

* An administrator takes moderation action affecting the contributor profile.
* A profile-related issue requires contributor attention.

Routine contributor profile edits by the contributor do not require notifications to the same contributor.

NGOs do not need to be notified for every profile edit by an applicant or assigned contributor in the MVP.

Contributor notifications for active project events, such as assignment, deliverable approval, deliverable rejection, project archival, or project completion, are defined in the relevant workflow modules rather than in the profile module.

MVP notifications may be in-platform only.

Email notifications are Post-MVP unless explicitly introduced later.

---

## Constraints

Contributor profiles must respect the following constraints:

* Contributor profiles belong to Contributor accounts only.
* Contributor skills, when selected, must come from the centralized skills catalog.
* Contributors cannot create arbitrary skills.
* Contributors are not required to select at least one skill before applying to projects.
* Availability is informational and must not impose artificial participation limits.
* Guests cannot view private contributor profiles.
* NGOs can view contributor profiles only in relevant application or assignment contexts.
* Profile edits must not rewrite historical workflow records.
* Public contributor profiles are not part of the MVP unless explicitly introduced later.
* Profiles must support skills-based contribution, not credential-based hiring.
* Contributor profiles must not turn SkillMatch into a job marketplace, freelance marketplace, or social network.
