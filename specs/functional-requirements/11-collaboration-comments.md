# 11. Collaboration Comments

## Purpose

Collaboration Comments provide lightweight, contextual communication around SkillMatch projects and deliverables.

Comments help NGOs and contributors coordinate work, clarify expectations, preserve decisions, and maintain accountability without turning SkillMatch into a general-purpose messaging or project management platform.

Comments must remain tied to specific collaboration contexts.

---

## Comment Contexts

MVP comments may exist in the following contexts:

* Project comments
* Deliverable comments

Application messages, deliverable submission comments, deliverable rejection feedback, and verification feedback are defined in their respective workflow modules.

Direct messages, private inboxes, real-time chat, typing indicators, read receipts, group chat, and social messaging features are out of scope for the MVP.

---

## Comment Ownership

Each comment belongs to:

* One author
* One context
* One timestamped collaboration record

The comment author may be a Contributor, NGO user, or Administrator depending on context and permissions.

Comments must preserve author identity for accountability.

Comments should not be reassigned to another author.

---

## Project Comment Rules

Project comments support coordination between the owning NGO and the assigned contributor after assignment.

Project comments may be used for:

* Scope clarification
* Coordination questions
* Lightweight progress updates
* Acknowledging deliverable changes after assignment
* Explaining issues that may lead to archival or cancellation

Project comments must remain tied to the project.

Project comments must not become general direct messaging between users.

Before assignment, project communication should happen through applications, project information, or other defined workflows rather than open project comments.

---

## Deliverable Comment Rules

Deliverable comments support discussion around a specific deliverable.

Deliverable comments may be used for:

* Clarifying submitted work
* Asking follow-up questions
* Explaining review concerns
* Supporting resubmission after rejection
* Preserving context around approval or rejection

Deliverable submission comments are required by the Deliverables module.

Deliverable rejection feedback is required by the Deliverables module.

Deliverable comments must remain tied to the deliverable and must not replace external project asset storage.

---

## Comment Visibility Rules

Project comments are visible to:

* The owning NGO
* The assigned contributor
* Administrators when required for governance, moderation, support, or operational integrity

Deliverable comments are visible to:

* The owning NGO
* The assigned contributor
* Administrators when required for governance, moderation, support, or operational integrity

Guests must not view project or deliverable comments.

Contributors who are not assigned to the project must not view project or deliverable comments.

NGOs that do not own the project must not view project or deliverable comments.

Comments must not appear in public project details, public project discovery, public NGO profiles, or public impact information.

---

## Comment Authoring Rules

The owning NGO may write comments on its own in-progress projects and deliverables.

The assigned contributor may write comments on assigned in-progress projects and deliverables.

Administrators may write or moderate comments only when there is a clear governance, safety, quality, abuse, fraud, support, or operational reason.

Guests must not write comments.

Contributors who are not assigned to the project must not write project or deliverable comments.

NGOs must not write comments on projects they do not own.

---

## Editing And Deletion Rules

MVP comments should be append-only for normal users.

Original authors should not edit or delete comments during normal workflow.

If a user needs to correct a comment, they should add a new comment with the correction.

Comment hiding or removal should be limited to administrative moderation, safety, abuse, privacy, or operational reasons.

Hidden or moderated comments should not erase the existence of relevant collaboration history when that history is needed for accountability.

The platform should avoid complex threaded comment management in the MVP.

---

## Moderation Rules

Administrators may moderate comments when required for platform safety, quality, abuse prevention, fraud prevention, support, or operational integrity.

Moderation may include hiding a comment, flagging a comment, or preserving an administrative note.

Moderation actions should be recorded for accountability.

Administrators must not use comment moderation to replace normal collaboration decisions.

---

## Activity History

Important comment events should be recorded when relevant for accountability or workflow traceability.

Activity history may include events such as:

* Project comment added.
* Deliverable comment added.
* Comment hidden or moderated by an administrator.
* Deliverable change acknowledged in project comments.

Comment activity should help reconstruct collaboration decisions without exposing private comments publicly.

The actor who performs an action does not need to receive a notification for that same action, but the action should be recorded when relevant for traceability.

---

## Notifications

The platform should notify relevant users when:

* The owning NGO adds a project comment that requires contributor attention.
* The assigned contributor adds a project comment that requires NGO attention.
* The owning NGO or assigned contributor adds a deliverable comment.
* A comment is moderated in a way that affects a participant.

Routine comments should notify the other relevant collaboration participant, not unrelated users.

The actor who writes a comment does not need to receive a notification for their own comment.

MVP notifications may be in-platform only.

Email notifications are Post-MVP unless explicitly introduced later.

---

## Constraints

Collaboration Comments must respect the following constraints:

* Comments must remain contextual to projects and deliverables.
* Comments must not become direct messages, real-time chat, or a general communication platform.
* Project and deliverable comments are private collaboration records, not public content.
* Only the owning NGO, assigned contributor, and authorized administrators may access project or deliverable comments.
* Comments must not expose private collaboration details in discovery, public project details, public NGO profiles, or impact information.
* Comments must preserve accountability and author identity.
* MVP comments are append-only for normal users.
* Comment moderation must be recorded when relevant.
* Threaded comments, read receipts, typing indicators, and direct messaging are out of scope for the MVP.
